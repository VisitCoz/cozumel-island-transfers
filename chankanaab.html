About Chankanaab National Park

Chankanaab sits inside Cozumel's National Reef Marine Park, about 5 miles south of the cruise port. The day combines a protected snorkeling reef, a dolphin and sea lion lagoon, botanical gardens, replica Mayan ruins, underwater statues, a small beach, and a tequila experience. Park entry covers the basics (chairs, hammocks, life vests, bathrooms, showers); activities like scuba ($45–65), Sea Trek ($84), and dolphin encounters ($109) are paid extras. Three oceanview restaurants on site.

Questions

Common questions about Chankanaab

What's included with park entry?

Parking, bathrooms, showers, dressing rooms, hammocks, beach chairs, life vests, the underwater statues, the inlet swimming area, Mayan replicas, tropical gardens, and the tequila experience. Snorkel rental is $15, scuba is $45–65, Sea Trek is $84, dolphin encounters from $109.

Hours?

Monday to Saturday, 8 AM to 4 PM. Closed Sundays.

How long should I plan for Chankanaab?

Most cruise guests spend 3–4 hours. Comfortably combines with downtown shopping if you book a later pickup.

Can I do the dolphin experience the same day?

Dolphin interactions sell out — book directly with the park ahead of your cruise day.
